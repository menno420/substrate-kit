### 💡 Session idea (dedup-checked against docs/ideas/ + roadmap)

**Surface the unresolved handoff at session START, not only at Stop.** Run-4's
core loss is that no measured session ever *opened* the auto-drafted card — the
kit's write-back machinery only speaks at Stop (auto-draft + advisory), i.e.
after the work, and nothing at session start says "you have an unresolved card /
here is the one-screen handoff." Concretely: the SessionStart hook (already
wired by `--wire-enforcement`) should inject a compact orientation block into the
session's opening context — the newest card (drafted or complete), its unresolved
slots, and the top of `docs/current-state.md` — turning the kit's continuity
surface from pull (hope the session reads docs) to push (it's in the first
screen). That directly attacks both run-4 findings: M2 (the card would be
impossible to miss) and M1 (injected context is cheaper than the exploratory
reading ON pays for today). Dedup: `t5-headless-guard-surface` is about hooks not
engaging in bench (now partially resolved); `substrate-kit-auto-drafted-handoff`
is the draft mechanism itself (shipped); no existing idea covers start-time
handoff injection. Run-3's still-unfiled "bench model-identity capture" idea also
remains worth filing, but this one is what run-4's evidence actually demands.
